Physiology Major, UCLA '27

Some of my favorite classes at UCLA so far.
Click any class to flip it.
Biomedical Ethics
  • Ethics in medicine, research, and end-of-life care
  • Left with more questions than answers

One of those classes that changes how you think. Worked through the hardest questions in medicine — consent, autonomy, end-of-life care, research ethics. The kind of class where you leave with more questions than answers, which means it did its job.

Principles of Oral Communication
  • Extemporaneous speech
  • Won extra credit for best speeches

Learned the art of extemporaneous speech — thinking on your feet, structuring an argument in real time, and delivering it with conviction. Won extra credit for best speeches. One of the most practically useful things I've learned at UCLA.

Economics 185 — Prof. Stephen Ross
  • Investing, markets, life
  • Drove from San Diego every evening to teach

Professor Ross drove down from San Diego every single evening to teach this class. His lectures were some of the most heartfelt I've ever heard — investing, markets, life. One of the best lessons I've ever learned came from this class:

"Lead with your heart. Tell people and show them."
Computer Science 97 — Entrepreneurship
  • Mini Y Combinator at UCLA
  • Won the pitch competition · built asksephira.com

Like a mini Y Combinator inside UCLA. Taught by Michael Blend (founder of Systems1 and MapQuest) and Josh Jones (founder of DreamHost and early Bitcoin investor) — genuinely extraordinary teachers and human beings.

We built asksephira.com and won the pitch competition.

PhysSci 107 — Systems Anatomy
  • The cadaver class — hallmark of the physiology major
  • Cranial nerves, every structure of the body

The hallmark of the physiology major at UCLA. Spent real time working with cadavers — an experience that taught me more than almost any other class in my life. Incredibly hard: tested on every cranial nerve, every intricate structure of the human body.

You come out of it with a completely different relationship to medicine.

Physics 5B — Thermodynamics & Waves
  • Heat systems, noise-cancelling headphones, blood vessels
  • Why ambulances sound different as they pass

One of the best professors I've had. Made physics feel like the explanation for everything: how heat systems work in houses, how noise-cancelling headphones work, the physics of blood vessels, why an ambulance sounds different as it passes.

The world makes more sense after this class.
